<p><p>Wheat heat treatment market is segmented into dry treatment and wet treatment types. Dry treatment involves applying heat directly to the wheat grain through methods like hot air treatments or infrared heating. Wet treatment, on the other hand, involves treating the wheat with steam or hot water before applying heat. Both methods aim to improve the quality of wheat by reducing microbial contamination, extending shelf life, and enhancing nutritional value. The choice between dry and wet treatment depends on factors like cost, efficiency, and desired end results.</p></p>
