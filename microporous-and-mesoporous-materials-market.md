<p><ul><li>Microporous Materials (Less than 2 nm)</li><li>Mesoporous Materials (2-50 nm)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Microporous materials have pore sizes of less than 2 nm, which are extremely small and can only allow small molecules to pass through. These materials are typically used in applications where precise control over molecule size and selectivity is required, such as in gas separation and molecular sieving. On the other hand, mesoporous materials have pore sizes ranging from 2 to 50 nm, allowing for larger molecules to enter. These materials find applications in catalysis, drug delivery, and adsorption processes.</p></p>
