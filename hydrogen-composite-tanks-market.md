<p><p>Hydrogen composite tanks are divided into two main market types: high pressure cylinders and low pressure cylinders. High pressure cylinders are designed to store hydrogen at a pressure of around 350-700 bar, making them suitable for applications requiring high storage capacity. Low pressure cylinders, on the other hand, store hydrogen at a pressure of less than 200 bar, making them more suitable for small-scale applications or where space is limited. Both types of tanks play a crucial role in enabling the safe storage and transportation of hydrogen for various industrial and energy purposes.</p></p>
