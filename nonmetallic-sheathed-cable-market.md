<p><p>Nonmetallic sheathed cable (NM cable) is a type of electrical wiring commonly used in residential construction. It consists of two or more insulated conductors housed in a nonmetallic sheath, typically made of PVC. NM cable is popular for its flexibility, easy installation, and cost-effectiveness, making it a preferred choice for wiring in homes and buildings.</p><p>The Nonmetallic Sheathed Cable Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.8% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to the increasing construction activities worldwide, especially in emerging economies, the rise in demand for electricity, and the implementation of stringent safety regulations governing electrical installations. Moreover, the development of smart homes and the increasing integration of renewable energy sources in buildings are also driving the demand for nonmetallic sheathed cables.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the nonmetallic sheathed cable market is the increasing adoption of higher quality and more durable materials in cable manufacturing to improve performance and longevity.
