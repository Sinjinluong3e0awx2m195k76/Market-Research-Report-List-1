<p><p>Mild-hybrid vehicles combine a traditional internal combustion engine with a small electric motor that assists in powering the vehicle. There are three main types of mild-hybrid vehicles: Series Hybrid Electric Vehicles (SHEV), which rely primarily on the electric motor for propulsion; Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEV), which can be charged externally and have a larger electric range; and Power-assist Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PSHEV), which use the electric motor to assist the engine during acceleration and cruising. Each type offers varying levels of fuel efficiency and environmental benefits.</p></p>
