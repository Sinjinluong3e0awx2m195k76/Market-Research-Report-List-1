<p><p>Industrial preservatives are commonly categorized into three types: Fatty preservatives, Inorganic Salt Preservatives, and Biological Preservatives. Fatty preservatives are derived from fatty acids and their esters, such as propionic acid. Inorganic salt preservatives include compounds like sodium nitrite and sodium benzoate. Biological preservatives consist of natural substances like bacteriocins and enzymes. Each type of preservative serves a specific function in inhibiting microbial growth and extending the shelf life of various industrial products.</p></p>
