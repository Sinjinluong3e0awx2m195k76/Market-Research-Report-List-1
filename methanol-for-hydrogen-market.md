<p><p>Methanol for hydrogen market is divided into two main types: Methanol Reforming and Methanol Decomposition. Methanol Reforming involves the conversion of methanol into hydrogen and carbon dioxide through a catalytic process. This method is widely used due to its efficiency and ability to produce high-purity hydrogen. On the other hand, Methanol Decomposition is the splitting of methanol molecules into hydrogen and carbon monoxide using high temperatures. This process is less common but offers potential for producing hydrogen without carbon dioxide emissions.</p></p>
