<p><p>Chickpea flour, also known as gram flour or besan, is available in two main varieties in the market: Desi and Kabuli. Desi chickpea flour is made from darker, smaller chickpeas with a higher fiber content, while Kabuli chickpea flour is made from larger, lighter-colored chickpeas with a milder flavor. Desi chickpea flour is commonly used in Indian and South Asian cuisines for dishes like pakoras and roti, while Kabuli chickpea flour is often used in Mediterranean dishes such as falafel and hummus. Both types of chickpea flour are versatile and nutritious alternatives to traditional wheat flour.</p></p>
