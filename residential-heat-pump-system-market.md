<p><p>Residential heat pump systems are categorized into three main types based on their heating source: electric heat pumps, gas heat pumps, and geothermal heat pumps. Electric heat pumps are the most common and utilize electricity to heat and cool homes. Gas heat pumps use natural gas as their heating source, providing an alternative to electric heat pumps. Geothermal heat pumps utilize the constant temperature of the earth to heat and cool homes, offering a more environmentally friendly and energy-efficient option for homeowners.</p></p>
