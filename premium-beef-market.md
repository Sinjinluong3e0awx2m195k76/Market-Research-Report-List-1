<p><p>Grainfed beef is produced from cattle that are fed a diet primarily consisting of grains in feedlots. This results in marbled, tender meat with a rich flavor. Grassfed beef, on the other hand, is derived from cattle that graze on pasture for most of their lives. This leads to leaner meat with a slightly gamier taste. Both grainfed and grassfed beef markets cater to different consumer preferences, with grainfed beef being more common and widely available in supermarkets, while grassfed beef is often found in specialty stores and farmers' markets.</p></p>
