<p><p>An Inferior Vena Cava (IVC) Filter is a medical device used to prevent blood clots from traveling to the lungs and causing a pulmonary embolism. It is implanted in the inferior vena cava, a large vein that carries deoxygenated blood from the lower body to the heart. The filter works by trapping blood clots and preventing them from reaching the lungs.</p><p>The global IVC Filter Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.1% during the forecast period. The increasing incidence of deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and pulmonary embolism, as well as the rising geriatric population, are driving the market growth. Additionally, advancements in technology leading to the development of more efficient and safer IVC filters are also contributing to the market expansion.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the IVC Filter market is the increasing preference for retrievable filters over permanent filters. Retrievable filters provide the option to remove the device once the risk of blood clot has passed, reducing the potential long-term risks associated with permanent filters. This trend is expected to drive market growth as it offers more flexibility and personalized treatment options for patients.</p></p>
