<p><p>Semi-automatic supermarket self-checkout machines require some level of input from the user, such as scanning items and completing payment. Fully automatic machines, on the other hand, operate without any assistance from the user once items are scanned. Both types aim to streamline the checkout process, reducing waiting times and increasing efficiency. Semi-automatic machines are more common in smaller retail stores, while fully automatic machines are prevalent in larger supermarkets with high volumes of customers.</p></p>
