<p><p>Static Var Compensators (SVCs) are devices used to regulate voltage on electrical power grids. There are two main types of SVCs on the market: Thyristor-Based SVCs and Magnetically Controlled Reactor (MCR)-Based SVCs. Thyristor-Based SVCs use thyristor valves to control the flow of reactive power, while MCR-Based SVCs use a reactor with a magnetic core to achieve the same goal. Both types of SVCs are essential in maintaining grid stability and ensuring efficient power transmission.</p></p>
