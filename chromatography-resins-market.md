<p><p>Chromatography resins can be categorized into two main types: natural resins and synthetic resins. Natural resins are derived from plant or animal sources and offer high selectivity and specificity in separating target molecules. On the other hand, synthetic resins are chemically manufactured and provide more uniform particle size distribution and higher mechanical stability. Both types of resins are widely used in chromatography processes for various applications in pharmaceutical, biotechnology, food and beverage, and other industries.</p></p>
