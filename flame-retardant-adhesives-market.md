<p><p>Flame retardant adhesives are categorized into two main types: Halogen-free flame retardant adhesives and Halogen flame retardant adhesives. </p><p>Halogen-free flame retardant adhesives do not contain halogenated compounds, offering a more environmentally friendly alternative that is less toxic when burned. On the other hand, Halogen flame retardant adhesives contain halogen elements such as chlorine or bromine, which are effective in reducing flammability but may release harmful gases when burned. Companies can choose between these two types based on their specific requirements and environmental considerations.</p></p>
