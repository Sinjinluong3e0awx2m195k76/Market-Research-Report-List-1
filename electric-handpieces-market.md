<p><p>Electric handpieces are dental instruments that provide more efficiency and precision during dental procedures. High-speed electric handpieces are designed for fast and accurate cutting, drilling, and shaping of teeth and bones. They operate at high rotational speeds, typically between 200,000 to 400,000 revolutions per minute (RPM). On the other hand, low-speed electric handpieces are used for slower and controlled procedures such as polishing and finishing restorations. These handpieces operate at lower speeds, generally ranging from 5,000 to 40,000 RPM. In summary, high-speed handpieces focus on quick and precise tasks, while low-speed handpieces are used for slower and detailed procedures.</p></p>
