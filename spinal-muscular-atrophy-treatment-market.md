<p><p>Spinal muscular atrophy (SMA) is a genetic disorder that affects the nerve cells controlling voluntary muscle movement. This deficiency results in the degeneration of motor neurons, leading to muscle weakness and atrophy.</p><p>The treatment landscape for spinal muscular atrophy has evolved significantly in recent years with the introduction of disease-modifying therapies. The most notable medication for SMA is nusinersen, a drug that helps increase production of the SMN protein by modifying the SMN2 gene.
