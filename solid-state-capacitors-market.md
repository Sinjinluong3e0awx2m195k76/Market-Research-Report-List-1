<p><ul><li>Inorganic Dielectric Capacitor</li><li>Organic Dielectric Capacitor</li><li>Electrolytic Capacitor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Solid-state capacitors can be classified into different types based on the dielectric material used. Inorganic dielectric capacitors utilize materials like ceramics or metal oxides for high reliability and stability. Organic dielectric capacitors use carbon-based materials for flexibility and cost-effectiveness. Electrolytic capacitors employ a liquid or gel electrolyte for high capacitance and low ESR. Each type of capacitor has its own advantages and disadvantages, catering to different applications based on factors like capacitance, voltage rating, and temperature stability.</p></p>
